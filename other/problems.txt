# NPC AI Requirements

## 1) Default States

Every NPC has a default state set at spawn. It is either **Idle/Guard** (returns to spawn and watches) or a **Schedule** (repeating route with tasks).

The default state lives at the bottom of the state stack and is never removed or replaced — only pushed over.

When all pushed states are popped, the NPC resumes its default state: continues on schedule or walks back to spawn and resumes look direction.

---

## 2) Suspicious Sightings (Location)

Something out of place but not immediately dangerous.

**Examples:** broken window, unknown sound (non-combat), suspicious item on the ground, activated distractor object.

**Rules:**
- Each suspicious location is investigated by exactly one NPC, exactly once.
- Before investigating, the NPC checks a global list of already-investigated or currently-investigated locations to avoid duplicates.
- The NPC only investigates if no active danger or responsible actor is visible.
- On arrival, the NPC looks around (~7 × 45° turns, updating FoV each time), then marks done and pops state.
- Not stored in personal knowledge — acted on directly and tracked via a global hash list only.

---

## 3) Suspicious Actor Sightings

An actor out of place or behaving strangely, not immediately threatening.

**Examples:** player in restricted zone, player openly carrying an illegal weapon, player near an active crime scene.

**Rules:**
- The NPC watches the actor and raises a suspicion meter over time.
- Raise rate depends on severity (hostile zone trespassing = fastest; open carry = slowest).
- If the actor fixes the condition before suspicion maxes, suspicion drains and Watch state is popped.
- If suspicion maxes, transitions to a Dangerous Actor reaction (§4).
- Only one NPC watches a given suspicious actor at a time.
- If the actor leaves FoV, the NPC chases briefly (~20 steps). No re-sighting → suspicion drains, Watch popped.

---

## 4) Dangerous Actor Sightings

The NPC has confirmed a hostile or overtly criminal actor.

**Rules:**
- **Guard:** immediately enters Combat.
- **Civilian (guard available):** runs to nearest guard and snitches.
- **Civilian (no guard):** panics and flees.
- If the dangerous actor is no longer visible, the Guard investigates the last known position before giving up.
- The sighting is stored in `LastSightingOfDangerous` and shared between guards via knowledge transfer.

---

## 5) Combat

Guards engage the dangerous actor.

**Rules:**
- Guard moves toward the target's last known position when out of sight.
- Guard fires when target is visible and projectile path is clear.
- Step-counter only increments when target is fully out of sight; resets whenever target is visible (even without a clear shot).
- ~50 consecutive steps with zero sighting → exit combat, investigate last known position.
- On exit: scheduled guards resume schedule; idle guards return to spawn.

---

## 6) Investigation

NPC walks to an incident location and searches the area.

**Rules:**
- On arrival, turns 45° at a time, updating FoV (~7 turns / ~3.5 s total).
- Environmental objects at the location (door, switch) may be interacted with.
- After looking around, incident is marked done and state is popped.
- If a new dangerous observation is made mid-investigation, the NPC reacts immediately.
- A location already being investigated by another NPC is skipped — no duplicates.

---

## 7) Cleanup

Guards clean up evidence in their map zone.

**Rules:**
- Bodies are bagged and dragged to the drop-off zone (if one exists).
- Illegal weapons/items are picked up from their exact location and secured.
- Blood is cleaned (stimulus removed from tile).
- Only incidents inside the guard's own zone are handled.
- After all tasks done, guard returns to default state.

---

## 8) Snitching (Civilian → Guard)

A civilian who witnessed danger and cannot fight runs to the nearest available guard.

**Rules:**
- Pathfinds to nearest available (non-downed, non-combat) guard.
- On arrival within 3 tiles with LoS, knowledge is transferred and the guard reacts.
- If snitching fails on arrival (guard moved), immediately replan to next nearest guard.
- If no guards exist, switch to Panic.
- After successful snitch, civilian's eyewitness flag is cleared.

---

## 9) Panic

Civilian or NPC with no other recourse after witnessing open violence.

**Rules:**
- Flees to the passable neighbor that maximizes summed distance from all danger locations.
- Threat actor (if present) weighted at 2× for flee direction.
- If no passable neighbor exists, wait one move cycle and retry.
- Panic lasts until popped externally (danger gone, script, etc.).

---

## 10) Knowledge & Alertness

### Personal Knowledge
- Each NPC holds a list of `IncidentReport` entries (type, location, tick).
- `LastSightingOfDangerous` is a dedicated single slot for the most recent confirmed dangerous actor.
- Handled incidents are marked and skipped; removed only when cleaned up.

### Knowledge Transfer
- Guards on the same team swap `LastSightingOfDangerous` — more recent tick wins.
- Suspicious location sightings are personal, never shared (global hash list prevents duplicates).

### Heightened Alertness — Alert Flag

A guard becomes **Alerted** when it processes a **Dangerous Sighting** (see §13).

Effects of the Alert flag:
- Suspicion meter in the Watch state fills at 2× the normal rate.
- Default movement speed is ~20% faster (move delay reduced from 0.8 s to ~0.64 s).
- The flag persists for the remainder of the mission once set.

---

## 11) NPC Reaction Matrix

| Situation                          | Guard                      | Civilian (guard nearby) | Civilian (no guard) |
|------------------------------------|----------------------------|-------------------------|---------------------|
| Suspicious location                | Investigate                | Ignore                  | Ignore              |
| Suspicious actor                   | Watch → Combat             | Watch → Snitch          | Watch → Panic       |
| Dangerous actor (seen)             | Combat                     | Snitch                  | Panic               |
| Dangerous actor (gone)             | Investigate LKP            | Snitch                  | Panic               |
| Dangerous location                 | Investigate                | Snitch                  | —                   |
| Dangerous sighting (no actor)      | AlarmRun / Shout+Investigate | —                     | —                   |

---

## 12) State Priority (high → low)

1. Combat / Frenzy
2. **AlarmRun**
3. Panic
4. Snitching
5. Watch (suspicious actor)
6. Investigation (dangerous location / actor LKP)
7. Investigation (suspicious location)
8. Cleanup
9. Scripted
10. **Default** (Schedule / Idle-Guard) — never popped

---

## 13) Dangerous Sightings (Location)

A dangerous sighting is evidence of serious crime with no obvious responsible actor currently visible.

**Examples:** an unattended corpse, a pool of blood with no suspect nearby, an explosion crater.

### Global tracking
- Dangerous sightings are **not** stored on individual actors.
- They are tracked in a **global map: `hash → investigator count`**.
- A sighting's hash encodes its type and location (same scheme as suspicious sightings).
- Up to **2 guards** may investigate the same dangerous sighting independently.
  A guard only claims a slot when it begins investigating; the counter is incremented then.

### Guard reaction
1. When a guard observes a dangerous sighting and the global count for that hash is < 2:
   - The guard registers itself (increments the counter).
   - Sets its own **Alert flag**.
   - **If the map has an alarm**: the guard enters **AlarmRun** state and moves to the nearest alarm to activate it.
   - **If no alarm exists**: the guard emits a shout sound event at regular intervals (~every 3 s) while investigating, alerting any guard within hearing range. Those guards also become Alerted and may claim an investigation slot.
2. If the global count is already 2, the guard ignores the sighting and continues its current state.

### Alarm objects
- Alarm objects are placed by map designers.
- Activating an alarm publishes a map-wide `AlarmTriggeredEvent`, which sets the Alert flag on every guard on the map.
- The last known position of the dangerous sighting is included in the event, allowing guards to investigate it if they weren't already aware.
- A guard heading for an alarm is in the **AlarmRun** state.
